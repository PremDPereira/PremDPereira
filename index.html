Renewable Energy source

Introduction

Renewable energy comes from natural sources that are replenished faster than
around us Unlike fossil fuels, which take millions of years to form and contribute to environmental degradation,
renewable energy sources offer sustainable alternatives that can significantly reduce greenhouse gas mitigate climate change

Types of Renewable Energy

  • Solar Energy
  • Wind Energy
  • Hydro Energy
  • Tidal Energy
  • Biomass Energy

Details

Solar energy

Solar energy is derived from the sun's radiation and is one of the most abundant and sustainable energy sources available on Earth.


Wind energy

Wind energy harnesses the kinetic energy of moving air by using large wind turbines
located on land (onshore) or in sea- or freshwater (offshore).



Hydro energy

hydro power is one of the most commercially developed.
By building a dam or barrier, a large reservoir can be used to create a controlled flow of water that will drive a turbine, generating electricity.



tidal energy

This is another form of hydro energy that uses twice-daily tidal currents to drive turbine generators.
